# Reference

## Course materials and inspirations

This course draws on, remixes, and extends materials from several open educational resources. We are grateful to the communities that created and maintain them.

### CodeRefinery
- **CodeRefinery**: https://coderefinery.org/
  Open teaching materials on version control, reproducible research, and software development for researchers. Many concepts in this course (cooking metaphors, reproducibility frameworks) are inspired by CodeRefinery lessons.
  - [Reproducible research lesson](https://coderefinery.github.io/reproducible-research/)
  - [Introduction to version control with Git](https://coderefinery.github.io/git-intro/)

### Research Data Management
- **RDMKit** (ELIXIR): https://rdmkit.elixir-europe.org/
  The Research Data Management toolkit for life sciences, providing guidance on all stages of the data lifecycle. The 7-stage lifecycle model (Plan, Collect, Process, Analyse, Preserve, Share, Reuse) in this course is based on RDMKit.

- **Wilkinson, M. D., et al. (2016)**. The FAIR Guiding Principles for scientific data management and stewardship. *Scientific Data*, 3, 160018. https://doi.org/10.1038/sdata.2016.18
  The original paper defining the FAIR data principles (Findable, Accessible, Interoperable, Reusable).

### Tidy Data
- **Wickham, H. (2014)**. Tidy Data. *Journal of Statistical Software*, 59(10), 1–23. https://vita.had.co.nz/papers/tidy-data.html
  The foundational paper defining tidy data principles. Highly readable and filled with practical examples.

- **Python for Scientific Computing** (Aalto Scientific Computing): https://aaltoscicomp.github.io/python-for-scicomp/
  Source of the file format comparison table in the tidy data section.

### Registered Reports
- **Chambers, C. D., & Tzavella, L. (2022)**. The past, present and future of registered reports. *Nature Human Behaviour*, 6, 29–42. https://doi.org/10.1038/s41562-021-01193-7
  History and rationale of the registered report publication format.

- **Center for Open Science**: https://www.cos.io/initiatives/registered-reports
  Detailed guide to registered reports, including a list of participating journals.

- **Glerean, E. (2022)**. Introduction to Registered Reports. https://hackmd.io/@eglerean/RegRep

### Computing and HPC
- **Aalto Scientific Computing**: https://scicomp.aalto.fi/
  Documentation and training materials for researchers using scientific computing at Aalto University. Includes the Shell Crash Course referenced in the course.

- **CSC — IT Center for Science (Finland)**: https://docs.csc.fi/
  User documentation for CSC's supercomputers (Puhti, Mahti, LUMI) and data services.

- **SURF (Netherlands)**: https://servicedesk.surf.nl/wiki/display/WIKI/Snellius
  Documentation for the Snellius national supercomputer.

- **NeIC — Nordic e-Infrastructure Collaboration**: https://neic.no/
  Nordic collaboration on research infrastructure, computing, and data services.

- **EuroHPC Joint Undertaking**: https://eurohpc-ju.europa.eu/
  European high-performance computing infrastructure, including LUMI and Leonardo.

- **"How to ask for help with (super)computers"**: https://zenodo.org/records/8392763
  Practical guide for researchers seeking technical support.

### Responsible AI and Ethics
- **AI Act (European Union, 2024)**: https://digital-strategy.ec.europa.eu/en/policies/regulatory-framework-ai
  The EU's regulatory framework for AI systems, including requirements for high-risk applications.

- **Responsible AI practices** (Google): https://ai.google/responsibility/responsible-ai-practices/
  Practical guidance on bias, fairness, and transparency in AI systems.

- **Large Language Models — responsible use in research** (Aalto University Research Services): https://zenodo.org/records/14032261
  Introduction to LLMs with focus on responsible research conduct.

### Data Visualization
- **R Graph Gallery**: https://r-graph-gallery.com/
  Comprehensive catalog of visualization types with R code examples.

- **Weissgerber, T. L., et al. (2015)**. Beyond Bar and Line Graphs: Time for a New Data Presentation Paradigm. *PLOS Biology*, 13(4), e1002128. https://doi.org/10.1371/journal.pbio.1002128
  Influential paper on why bar charts can be misleading and what to use instead.

### Data Cleaning
- **Au, R. (2019)**. "Why is data cleaning hard to teach?" https://www.counting-stuff.com/whys-it-hard-to-teach-data-cleaning-65949948516031001b3512c8/
  Thoughtful reflection on the context-dependent nature of data preprocessing.

### Scientific Software and Training
- **The Carpentries**: https://carpentries.org/
  Community-driven workshops on software and data skills for researchers. Includes Software Carpentry, Data Carpentry, and Library Carpentry.

- **Research Software Engineering community**: https://society-rse.org/
  International community for research software engineers. National chapters exist in many countries.
